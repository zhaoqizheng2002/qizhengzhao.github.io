01 // About

Research Overview

I am a researcher working at the intersection of health informatics, machine learning, and epidemiology. My primary focus is building intelligent systems for predictive health management — particularly fall risk assessment and geriatric care — using multimodal deep learning and sensor fusion.

With a clinical background in preventive medicine (Sun Yat-sen University) and graduate training in health informatics at Karolinska Institutet & Stockholm University, I bridge the gap between data-driven AI and real-world clinical decision support.

My published work spans transformer architectures for skeleton-based fall prediction, multimodal IMU-image fusion networks, global disease burden analysis using GBD data, and graph neural networks for epidemiological nowcasting. I have served as Principal Investigator on a nationally-funded research project on gait-based risk identification.
